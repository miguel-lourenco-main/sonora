14. Science Explainer
-------------------------------------------

"Have you ever wondered how the northern lights create their dazzling display? The aurora borealis is a spectacular natural phenomenon caused by charged particles from the sun interacting with Earth’s magnetic field. When these solar particles collide with gases in our atmosphere, they excite the atoms, causing them to emit light. The colors of the aurora depend on the type of gas and altitude of the collision – oxygen produces green and red, while nitrogen gives us purple and blue hues. This cosmic dance is not just beautiful but also a reminder of the dynamic forces shaping our universe."
